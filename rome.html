Rome, Italy – A Living Museum

Rome is history in motion. Every corner of the city tells a story, and what makes it even more fascinating is how seamlessly ancient ruins blend into modern life.

You can walk past the Colosseum, then turn a corner and find yourself in a lively street filled with restaurants and locals enjoying their day. The contrast is surreal—thousands of years of history existing alongside everyday life.

The Vatican adds another layer of depth, with breathtaking art and architecture that feels almost overwhelming in its scale. And yet, Rome never feels too formal. It’s alive, chaotic, and full of personality.

Food plays a huge role here. From fresh pasta to authentic pizza, meals feel less like eating and more like an event. There’s a warmth in Roman culture that makes you feel welcomed, even as a visitor.
