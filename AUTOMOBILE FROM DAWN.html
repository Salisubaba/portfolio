LEONARDO DA VINCI-Leonardo da Vinci invented the first self-propelled car in 1478. However, the car is sketched on paper and is never actually made. This self propelled car is not similar to the cars we see today. It's more like a cart and does not have seats. The sketch is of extreme importance considering it depicts the plan of the first ever self-propelled vehicle in the world , confirming Leonardo da Vinci as the inventor of the earliest form of automobile.

Leonardo da Vinci's sketch is of an open top three wheeler, capable of motion whithout being pushed. The sketch is scaled to a cart measuring 1.68 meters wide (or 5 foot 6 inches by 4 foot 11 inches) and ranks at the very top of Leonardo da Vinci's innovations related to transportation and locomotion.

The cart is depicted to run on clockwork, powered by the coiled leaf springs.Leonardo da Vinci's sketched work was crafter in 2004 and its displayed in the institute and museum of history of science in florence, italy.

The story of the automobile is far more than a timeline of engines and aesthetics; it is a testament to human ingenuity and our restless desire to bridge the distance between where we are and where we want to be. From the first rhythmic thrum of a Benz Patent-Motorwagen to the near-silent acceleration of today’s electric motors, the car has reshaped our cities, our economies, and our very sense of freedom. As we look toward a future defined by autonomy, sustainability, and renewed connectivity, one thing remains certain: the journey is far from over. The automobile will continue to evolve, proving that while the technology may change, our drive to explore the horizon remains universal.
